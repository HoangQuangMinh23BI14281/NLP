\section{Introduction}

Online recruitment platforms have produced a large number of job descriptions (JDs). These postings contain useful information such as job role, required skills, location, experience level, and salary. However, this information is usually written in free-form text and the format often changes between companies. Because of this, it is difficult and time-consuming to manually read and extract important information from job postings. In many applications such as job recommendation, resume matching, and labor market analysis, it is helpful to convert raw JD text into structured data. Named Entity Recognition (NER) is a task in Natural Language Processing (NLP) that identifies important pieces of information in text and assigns them to predefined categories. In the recruitment domain, NER can help convert unstructured job descriptions into structured fields. However, this task is challenging because job postings use different writing styles, abbreviations, and domain-specific terms. Some information, such as salary or experience requirements, can also appear in many different forms.

In this project, we study the task of extracting information from job descriptions using Transformer-based NER models. Our goal is to detect five types of entities that are important in recruitment systems: ROLE, SKILL, LOC, EXP, and SALARY. We fine-tune several pre-trained Transformer models, including BERT, DistilBERT, and RoBERTa, and compare different training methods and decoding strategies. To evaluate the models, we build a custom dataset with token-level labels following the IOB tagging scheme. The dataset contains job descriptions from different technical domains. We train and evaluate the models using common NER metrics such as precision, recall, and F1-score. The results help us understand how well different models and training methods perform on the task of extracting structured information from job descriptions.
